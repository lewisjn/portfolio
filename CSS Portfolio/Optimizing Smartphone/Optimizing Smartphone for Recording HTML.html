\ Before You Start\

\
\
\
\
\

Do you have enough storage?

\

\f1 \uc0\u55357 \u56561 \f0

\

\ Video and audio files can be large! Before you start, make sure you have space on your phone to record and save files. If you need to free up space on your phone, you can back up your files to your computer or the cloud through Google Drive or Dropbox. \
\
\

\
\
\
\
\

Is your phone charged?

\

\f1 \uc0\u55357 \u56587 \f0

\

\ Recording video can rapidly drain your battery life. Make sure your battery is charged before you start shooting video. To be safe, bring a charging cable or a portable battery with you. Warning: \ Recording while plugged into a power source can sometimes cause a strange buzzing sound in the recording.\

\
\
\
\
\

Is your phone in airplane mode?

\

\f1 \uc0\u9992 \u65039 \f0

\

\ Airplane mode will reserve your battery life and prevent notifications and distractions while recording. If your phone is in airplane mode, you do not have to worry about an accidental message alert coming through and appearing in your recording. It also can improve the audio quality of the recording.\
\ \

\
\
\
\
\
\
\

Recording on a Smartphone

\

\ \ If you are recording audio only, consider using an app that only captures audio. There are a couple of free phone apps that can give you a bit more control over your recording or you can use your default Voice Memo app, which is pretty user-friendly.\ \

\

\ \ \ Awesome Voice Recorder (iPhone) | Hi Q Voice Recorder (Android) \ \ \

\

\ \ When recording, make sure to record a little bit before and after the action you want to capture. \ This creates a header\ (empty space at the beginning) and a tail\ (empty space at the end). \ There is a small lag between hitting the record button and the recording actually starting. \ This space ensures that you don’t miss anything an\ d will make editing easier.\ \

\

\
\

\f1 \uc0\u55357 \u56586 \f0 Quick Tips for Recording Audio \f1 \uc0\u55357 \u56586 \f0

\
    \
  • \ Quiet Location - \ Enclosed, indoors spaces and with carpeted areas are the best. Consider recording in a closet, under a blanket or both! You can also record audio in your car. \ \
    • \
  • \ Microphone Position\ - Your mic is located near the bottom of the phone (not the top!) \ \
    • \
  • \ Listening - \ trust your ears! If you hear something weird, so did your phone. \ \
    • \
\

\f1 \uc0\u55356 \u57253 \f0 Quick Tips for Recording Video \f1 \uc0\u55356 \u57253 \f0

\
    \
  • \ Quick Tap\ - Tapping the screen adjusts brightness and focus for that area.\ \
    • \
  • \ Tap and Hold \ - Locks the brightness and focus for that part of the frame, so it doesn’t change when you move around.\ \
    • \
  • \ Vertical vs. Horizontal \ - Decide what orientation you want to shoot your video in. Most video editors are designed for horizontal video. Vertical videos are designed for cellphones.\ \
    • \

Transferring Files to a Computer

\

\f1 \uc0\u55357 \u56561 \u10145 \u65039 \u55357 \u56507 \f0

\
    \
  • \ Sharing via the cloud:\ \  Upload or backup your files to Google Drive or Dropbox.\
    • \
  • \ Sharing via USB/USB-C: \ \ P\ lug your smartphone directly into your computer and move files via File Explorer (PC) or Finder (Mac).\
    • \
  • \ Auto-transferring issues: \ Most computers have a default Photos/Movies application that will automatically detect your files, but they can be hard to find later if you’re not familiar with it.\ \
    • \
  • \ A note about sharing via text: \ in some cases this might compress the files and reduce their quality.\ \
    • \
  • \ In a pinch: \ emailing the file to yourself can work!\ \
    • \
